How did you become a web developer

A few months before finishing my degree, I took a free coding course online to learn about the basics of web development. Several weeks later I was hooked, and I instinctively knew that I wanted to pursue it further. However, my focus was on my final exams and securing a graduate job, so web development took a back seat.

Fast forward 18 months, after stints in accounting and financial services, I took the plunge and enrolled in an online bootcamp in February 2018, taking the next step towards pursuing web development as a career. I completed the bootcamp in August 2018.

What is your development process

I start by understanding the requirements of the end product, and then I develop ideas around those core requirements. For example, I might frame a project according to the considerations below:

  • Minimal vs. Detailed
  • Mobile-first vs. Responsive
  • End user demographics

Once I have a clear direction for the functional aspect, the next stage is aesthetics. Most of my websites use a hero image as the focal point to draw in users, so I tend to start my design process by finding an appropriate image, and then afterwards I formulate the pallette and typography to fit in and create an overall theme. I also browse the websites of similar clients or those aimed at a similar target audience, taking note of prevalent design aspects or features which appear to be popular, as often there is much to be learned from other successful projects.

What do you most/least enjoy about web development

Thinking of an idea and then seeing it come alive on the screen in front of you is probably the thing I enjoy most about web development. I enjoy having the freedom to harness all of my creativity and imagination to achieve an end result that is not only good on the eye but also functional and which serves a purpose for a client.

My least favourite aspect is the time spent troubleshooting and debugging. I have quickly learned that, no matter how careful you are at the outset, inevitably you will have to set aside time to solve unforeseen issues and bugs. Having said that, it's a great feeling when you eventually crack them!
